Living well

You can't undo a diagnosis. You can take charge of what comes next.

Three things gave me back a sense of control: eating a plant-based way that nourishes my liver, understanding my own test results, and learning to truly partner with my care team. Here's what I've learned, shared to help you feel a little more in charge, too.

01 · A plant-based lifestyle

Food and habits that love your liver back.

For me, eating plant-based isn't about restriction, it's about filling my plate with the foods that help my liver most: fiber-rich vegetables, legumes, whole grains, fruit, nuts, and seeds. Alongside food, gentle daily movement, good sleep, and managing stress all became part of taking care of this quiet, hardworking organ.

🌱 Eat the rainbow

Vegetables, legumes, whole grains, and fruit at the center of every plate.

🚫 Ease off

Added sugar, refined carbs, ultra-processed foods, and alcohol.

🚶 Keep moving

Regular, sustainable activity you can actually keep up.

😴 Rest & reset

Sleep and stress care are part of liver care, too.

A plant-based diet can be wonderfully nourishing, but everyone's needs differ, a registered dietitian can help you plan for nutrients like vitamin B12, vitamin D, iron, and omega-3s, and tailor an eating pattern to your health.
02 · Know your liver tests

Your numbers, in plain language.

Liver disease is often silent, so tests are how we see what's really happening. You don't need to memorize the science, but knowing the names helps you ask for them and understand your results.

ALT & AST

Liver enzymes measured in a routine blood test. When liver cells are stressed or inflamed, these can rise, an early flag worth watching.

GGT, ALP & bilirubin

Additional blood markers that help your doctor see how the liver and bile ducts are functioning.

Platelet count & albumin

Part of routine bloods; changes over time can hint at how the liver is coping.

FIB-4 & APRI scores

Simple calculations from your existing blood results that estimate the likelihood of scarring (fibrosis), no extra needle required.

FibroScan (elastography)

A quick, painless scan that measures liver stiffness and fat, a common way to assess fibrosis without a biopsy.

Ultrasound & MRI

Imaging that can show fat and changes in the liver; MR elastography and MRI-PDFF give detailed pictures when needed.

These descriptions are general and educational. Which tests are right for you, and what your results mean, is a conversation for you and a qualified healthcare professional.
03 · Talk with your care team

You are a partner in your care, not a passenger.

The most important thing I learned: it's okay to ask questions, take notes, and ask again until you understand. Come prepared, keep your own copy of your results, and don't be afraid to ask for the right specialists.

  • ✓ Bring a written list of questions and symptoms.
  • ✓ Keep your own folder of lab results and scans.
  • ✓ Ask about a hepatologist and a registered dietitian.
  • ✓ Bring someone you trust if it helps you feel heard.

Questions worth asking

"Given my risk factors, should my liver be assessed?"
"What do my ALT and AST results mean for me?"
"Would a FibroScan or a FIB-4 score help check for scarring?"
"What changes would help my liver the most right now?"
"Should I see a hepatologist or a dietitian?"
