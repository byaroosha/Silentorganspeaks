My story

I didn't feel sick. That's the terrifying part.

For years my liver quietly carried the weight — no pain, no warning. By the time I had answers, the disease had a name most people had never heard: MASH.

What started as one frightening diagnosis became a decision: to learn everything, to change how I live, and to stand on stages telling the truth so the next person catches it sooner. This is that story — and it's still being written.

Why it matters

A silent condition, hiding in plain sight

Metabolic dysfunction–associated steatohepatitis (MASH) is one of the fastest-growing liver conditions in the world — and most people who have it don't know yet.

~1 in 3

adults worldwide are estimated to live with metabolic-associated fatty liver disease (MASLD).

Often 0

obvious symptoms in the early stages — which is why it earns the name "silent."

Reversible

early liver damage can often be improved through timely care and lifestyle change.
